# Customer Churn Analysis

## Project Overview

Customer churn is one of the biggest challenges for telecom companies because losing customers directly affects revenue and growth. In this project, I analyzed a telecom customer churn dataset to understand customer behavior, identify factors that contribute to churn, and generate insights that can help improve customer retention.

The analysis was performed using Python in Google Colab and focuses on finding patterns in customer demographics, services, billing information, and customer status.

---

## Objective

* Identify the key factors influencing customer churn.
* Understand customer behavior towards telecom services.
* Generate insights that can help businesses reduce customer attrition.
* Provide practical recommendations based on the analysis.

---

## Dataset Information

* Dataset: Telecom Customer Churn Dataset
* Source: Kaggle
* Records: 7,043 customers
* Features: 38 columns

The dataset contains information related to customer demographics, services subscribed, billing details, contract types, and churn status.

---

## Key Findings

* Most customers remain with the company, but a significant number have churned.
* Customers with month-to-month contracts are more likely to leave than customers on long-term contracts.
* Age and gender show very little impact on churn behavior.
* Customers paying higher monthly charges tend to churn more frequently.
* Unmarried customers and customers without dependents show higher churn rates.
* Customers using internet services have higher churn rates than those who do not.
* Fiber optic users are more likely to churn compared to customers using other internet types.
* Contract type, tenure, and offer type are among the strongest factors affecting churn.

---

## Business Recommendations

* Introduce affordable plans for price-sensitive customers.
* Encourage month-to-month customers to switch to longer contracts through incentives and discounts.
* Improve customer support and service experience.
* Launch stronger referral programs to increase customer engagement.
* Review offers that receive poor customer response and make improvements based on customer feedback.


---

## Conclusion

The analysis shows that customer churn is influenced more by business-related factors such as contract type, tenure, internet service, and monthly charges than by demographic factors. By focusing on customer retention strategies and improving service offerings, telecom companies can reduce churn and improve customer satisfaction.
